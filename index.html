August 17 in Hamburg

UX Camp Hamburg is returning this summer. After an exciting first un-conference last year we are in the middle of planning for the second iteration this August 17. It's happening all again: It will be a one day event. It will take place on a saturday. It will be hosted by our location sponsor Sinnerschrader where you can have outside sessions for some blue sky thinking. It is an all-inclusive-deal. And the best: It is FREE!

The day will look similar to this planned scenario:
  • Kicking off at 9AM, registration starts and you get to acclimatise yourself with some nice tea or coffee.
  • Close to 10AM we will start with getting to know each other and the session plannning.
  • At 11AM four parallel sessions will commence in well equipped conference rooms or totally analog outside on the lawn.
  • A full lunch on site will be served for both vegetarians and carnivores at around 1PM.
  • Tea time and possible repeat sessions can happen after 4PM
  • Depending on sessions we will slide into BBQ and beers by about six.
Volunteers unite!

Of course all this is not possible without YOU! (Yes, we are looking at you!) A vital part of any bar camp are its volunteers. A few are needed during planning time, more during the event. Helping with setting up the rooms, food and bevs, handling the BBQ and many other tidbits. Please get in touch at orga[at]uxcamphh.org. We love you for it.

Getting Started

BarCamp is an international network of user-generated conferences (or unconferences) primarily focused around technology and the Web. They are open, participatory workshop-events, the content of which is provided by participants. Attendees schedule sessions by writing on a whiteboard or putting a Post-It note on a 'grid' of sessions. Unlike traditional conference formats, BarCamps have a self-organizing character, relying on the passion and the responsibility of the participants. All attendees are encouraged to present or facilitate a session or otherwise contribute to the event. Source: Wikipedia
